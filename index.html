LIGHTNING LAB DIGITAL ACCELERATOR | WELLINGTON

March 2014 - July 2014

Founder

I was selected as one of 29 entrepreneurial Company Founders to partake in the second intake of New Zealand’s only startup accelerator. Lightning Lab takes on 10 young companies and provides investment and twelve weeks of intense direction to help them raise seed capital on Demo Day, at the completion of the programme.

My team and I founded TradeIntel, an analytics platform for Trade Me, New Zealand’s online auction platform. We provided information & insight to large sellers on competitive factors, and changes in demand across the categories of goods sold on the site. My roles were mostly customer and product oriented: I owned Sales & Marketing, and was responsible for our user-experience and front-end design. In constantly communicating with our early adopters, I ensured we were building a platform that our converted customers would use, and keep using. This involved sustained communication with customers and the team, and management of feature rollout across our userbase. MVP testing and fast iteration allowed us to transform our clunky, subscription-based web system into a seamless, pay-per-use report delivery service, providing more value to our customers and increasing revenue.

Unfortunately, a failure to reach a commercial agreement over the use of auction data lead to the eventual shutdown of the company.

Responsible for the daily operations of Wellington’s largest A La Carte fine dining restaurant. Management of stock, staff, and customers in a high quality, high pressure, high turnover environment. The size of the establishment and the scale of the team demanded expertise, diplomacy, and constant communication - at which I excelled.

Managing every detail of the customer experience from end-to-end, and working with passionate, talented people is what really drove me in this role. From the moment customers picked up the phone to make a reservation, till they posted a great review, providing a consistently high level of service was the number-one priority. In the interests of continuous improvement I implemented several internal initiatives, including composing menu glossaries and wine tasting notes to upskill and retain talented staff.

I cross-credited to a Software Development Diploma at Weltec which I completed while working full time.
