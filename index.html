LIGHTNING LAB DIGITAL ACCELERATOR | WELLINGTON

March 2014 - July 2014

Founder

I was selected as one of 29 entrepreneurial Company Founders to partake in the second intake of New Zealand’s only startup accelerator. Lightning Lab takes on ten young companies and provides investment and twelve weeks of intense direction to help them raise seed capital on Demo Day, at the completion of the programme.

My team and I founded TradeIntel, an analytics platform for Trade Me, New Zealand’s online auction platform. We provided information & insight to large sellers on competitive factors, and changes in demand across the categories of goods sold on the site. My roles were mostly customer and product oriented: I owned Sales & Marketing, and was responsible for our user-experience and front-end design. Through constant communication with our early adopters, I ensured we were building a platform that our converted customers would use, and keep using. This involved sustained communication with customers and the team, and management of feature rollout across our userbase. MVP testing and fast iteration allowed us to transform our clunky, subscription-based web system into a seamless, pay-per-use report delivery service, providing more value to our customers and increasing revenue.

It was here that I learned the importance of validating my assumptions when building products, and that there is no substitute for getting out of the building and into the real world. Ultimately, a failure to reach a commercial agreement with Trade Me over the use of auction data lead to the shutdown of the company.
